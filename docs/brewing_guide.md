## Coffee-to-Water Ratio
A standard pour-over uses a 1:16 ratio, and a stronger cup uses 1:15. Espresso is far more concentrated at roughly 1:2.

## Water Temperature
Brew between 195F and 205F. Cooler water under-extracts and tastes sour; hotter water over-extracts and tastes bitter.

## Grind Size
Match the grind to the method: fine for espresso, medium for pour-over, coarse for French press. A too-fine French press grind tastes muddy.

## Water Quality
Use filtered water with moderate mineral content, around 150 ppm total dissolved solids. Distilled water tastes flat because it lacks the minerals that carry flavor, and very hard water can leave scale and mute acidity.

## Storing Beans
Keep beans in an airtight container away from light and heat, and grind just before brewing. Freezing works for long-term storage but avoid repeated thawing.
